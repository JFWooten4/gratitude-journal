Been just over a week since I dropped in here, and what a great week it's been! Had an awesome chat with James last night, and I'm so thankful to find these incredible self-organizing and self-incentivized communities around building a better market. I've also had quite the incredible past birthday weekend. First we had Dad on Saturday, followed by Aunt Patty on Sunday. I gave them both quick calls, and they were just so sweet and happy to chat.

I hope Dad's having a fun time skiing in faraway land. I really wanted to plan a whole trip out to Denver to use the last couple days at some of the big Rockies resorts, and I love and cherish those memories from when I was growing up. Hopefully I'll have the leeway to enjoy a lot more of that when I grow up (i.e. make enough to support Kayla and myself). A few outstanding paid things to do, like the Stellar grant, but I've sort of humorously noticed that I just couldn't care less about the centralized client relationships that pay the corporate money.

I read someone's page the other day, a person who did the Reinventing Orgs video series. They said the business model just doesn't feel right, and I think that's how I approach a lot of public-utility banking services. These things just need to exist and function for the use of all without immense conflicting interests and legacy business models. But hey, I'm looking out one of the most gorgeous windows of my life right now, and I suppose it's time to appreciate that.

I went on a walk the other day for a couple hours, down the road and to the rail trail. But this time I ventured past the terminus, down to the river. I found a fallen tree overhanging the creek and sat there for a while, enjoying the bliss of natural serenity all around me. I heard water streaming by across the rocks while birds cawed in the distance. It was so silent and untouched, with not a single city or car noise to be heard. The farms nearby were distant thoughts, with pristine landscapes flowing endlessly into the river.

It was nice to sit and be one with nature and its desires momentarily. So much of our world orbits around these decentralized autonomous systems, and I wonder just how incredible a future might be where we can offer these special moments at nominal cost to anyone in the economy. _The economy_, what a fun way to look at all work.
